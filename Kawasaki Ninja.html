kawasaki Ninja H2R

kawasaki Ninja H2R

Price: ₹1600/day

Overview:The Kawasaki Ninja H2 is a supercharged four-stroke supersport-class motorcycle in the Ninja sports bike series manufactured by Kawasaki, featuring a variable-speed centrifugal supercharger. Its namesake is the 750 cc Kawasaki H2 Mach IV an inline triple that was introduced by Kawasaki in 1972 to "disrupt what it saw as a sleeping motorcycle market".

Kawasaki selected the literbike platform for its top-of-the-line Ninja H2 model, rather than continuing with the higher-displacement Ninja ZX-14 hyperbike. the center of the high-performance market", attracting the best development in racing, with the best chassis and suspension design, so it made sense for Kawasaki to create a machine that could leverage this. The H2 is the first production motorcycle with a supercharger, although turbochargers were available on some models in the early 1980s. Specifications in the infobox are from Kawasaki unless noted.

The front fairing of the Ninja H2R incorporates winglets made of carbon fiber, just like the rest of the H2R-exclusive bodywork. They may be aerodynamic devices designed to create a low-pressure zone to help move cooling air through the engine bay, to produce downforce at high speed, or to provide straight-line stability in a short-wheelbase sportsbike chassis.

The motorcycle features a trellis frame made of high-tensile steel, with a swingarm mounting plate. Its rake and trail are 25.1° and 4.3 inches, respectively. The overall length is 81.5 inches, the width is 33.5 inches, and the overall height is 45.7 inches. The ground clearance stands at 5.1 inches, with a seat height of 32.7 inches. The dry weight of the motorcycle is 432.2 lbs, while the curb weight is 476.3 lbs. It has a fuel capacity of 4.5 gallons and a wheelbase of 57.1 inches.

Features: Its Ninja H2R track-only variant is the fastest and most powerful production motorcycle on the market, producing a maximum of 310 horsepower (230 kW) and 326 horsepower (243 kW) with ram-air. The H2R has 50% more power than the fastest street-legal motorcycles, while the street-legal Ninja H2 has a lower power output of 200 hp (150 kW)–210 hp (160 kW) with ram-air.

The motorcycle is equipped with a 120/60-17 V01F slick tire at the front and a 190/65-17 V01R slick tire at the rear. The front brakes consist of dual 330mm semi-floating discs paired with dual radial-mount, opposed 4-piston Brembo M50 Monobloc calipers, along with the Kawasaki Intelligent Anti-lock Brake System (KIBS). The rear brake features a single 250mm disc with an opposed 2-piston caliper, also utilizing the Kawasaki Intelligent Anti-lock Brake System (KIBS).

Special features include a supercharged engine, carbon fiber aerodynamic downforce-generating devices, and highly durable paint. The available color option is Mirror Coated Matte Spark Black. The motorcycle is powered by a 4-stroke, in-line 4-cylinder engine with DOHC, 16 valves, and liquid cooling. It has a displacement of 998cc, with a bore and stroke of 76.0 x 55.0mm and a compression ratio of 8.3:1.

The engine delivers a maximum horsepower of 321.5 hp at 14,000 rpm and a maximum torque of 121.5 lb-ft at 12,500 rpm. The fuel system includes DFI® with four 50mm throttle bodies and dual injection, along with a Kawasaki Supercharger. It features a TCBI ignition system with Digital Advance, and a 6-speed transmission with a return shift and dog-ring design. The final drive is a sealed chain.
